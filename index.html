Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our partnership model

How is this different from selling my business?

+

When you sell, you get a one-time payment and lose all control. With our model, you keep your baseline profit, maintain strategic oversight, and share in the multiplied value when we exit together. You're not giving up your business‚Äîyou're gaining partners who only earn when they create growth.

What happens if the 90-day trial doesn't work out?

+

Simple: we part ways with no hard feelings. If we don't meet the agreed KPIs after 90 days, you've only invested $2,000/month while we handled operations. Your baseline profit was protected throughout, and you can return to running your business or explore other options.

How much control do I maintain?

+

You maintain strategic oversight throughout the partnership. You have veto rights on major decisions, participate in quarterly board meetings, and set the overall direction. We handle day-to-day operations so you can focus on high-level strategy without being trapped in the business.

What if my business doesn't grow 2-3x?

+

Your baseline profit is always protected‚Äîyou keep receiving it regardless. We only earn equity from growth above your baseline. If we don't create significant growth, we don't earn significant equity. Our incentives are completely aligned with yours.

How is the baseline profit determined?

+

During the Discovery phase, we review your financials and agree on a baseline based on your average monthly profit over the past 6-12 months. This baseline is locked in and protected‚Äîyou continue receiving this amount throughout our partnership, regardless of short-term fluctuations.

What types of businesses do you work with?

+

We focus on service businesses and consumer brands in Singapore and Malaysia with $500K-$5M in annual revenue. Ideal businesses have proven product-market fit but are limited by the owner's time and operational capacity. We've successfully scaled businesses in retail, e-commerce, B2B services, and consumer products.

When do we exit and how is it decided?

+

Typically within 2-5 years, once we've grown the business 2-3x. The exit decision is made jointly based on market conditions, business performance, and both parties' goals. Options include selling to a strategic buyer, private equity, or a management buyout. You have equal say in the timing and terms of the exit.

What's the typical equity split?

+

This varies by business and is determined during the Discovery phase. Our equity is earned gradually based on the growth we create above your baseline. The formula is transparent and agreed upon upfront. Typically, we move toward majority ownership as we scale the business, but terms are customized to each situation.

Do you invest capital into the business?

+

We primarily invest our time, expertise, and networks. However, we can facilitate access to capital through our extensive network if growth requires additional funding. We have connections to debt, equity, and mezzanine financing sources and can help structure deals that make sense for the business.
